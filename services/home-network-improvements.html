Better home WiFi coverage, stable performance, and professionally planned UniFi networking.

Get a quoteHow Installation Works

A good home network should quietly support work, streaming, gaming, cameras, smart devices, and guests without requiring constant resets.

Why home WiFi falls short

ISP routers are often placed where the internet enters the house rather than where coverage is needed. Multiple floors, basements, garages, stone, brick, ductwork, and long floor plans can all weaken performance.

Wired access points instead of repeated signals

When possible, we use Ethernet backhaul so each access point has a stable connection to the network. This is generally more dependable than adding consumer extenders or relying on multiple wireless hops.

Choosing between U6 and U7

Newer is not automatically better. Certain U6 models remain excellent choices because of their radio capability, coverage, value, and compatibility. U7 models are useful where newer clients, 6 GHz, or future planning justify them.

Networks for real household use

We account for remote work, gaming systems, streaming TVs, smart-home devices, cameras, outdoor spaces, and guest access rather than designing only around a speed test beside the router.

Clean installation and handoff

Access points, switches, gateways, and cabling are installed neatly. The completed network is tested, named clearly, and reviewed with the homeowner.
