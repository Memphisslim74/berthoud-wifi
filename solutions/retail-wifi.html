Retail Store WiFi and Network Infrastructure

UniFi networking for retail stores, point-of-sale systems, staff devices, guest access, cameras, and inventory equipment.

Get a quoteInstallation Process

Retail networks support payment systems, inventory devices, printers, cameras, staff tablets, digital signage, and sometimes customer WiFi. Reliability and separation are essential.

Critical trafficPOS, payment, inventory, and back-office systems.
Security needSeparate operational, camera, staff, and guest networks.
CoverageSales floor, stockroom, office, entrances, and exterior.
GrowthAdditional registers, cameras, handheld devices, and locations.

Protect point-of-sale connectivity

Fixed registers, printers, and back-office devices should use stable wired connections where practical. Handheld devices need carefully planned WiFi coverage across the sales floor and stockroom.

Guest and personal devices should not share unrestricted access with payment or operational systems.

Coverage across changing layouts

Shelving, displays, inventory, refrigeration, walls, and seasonal layouts can change wireless behavior. Access points are placed and tuned around the actual retail environment.

Stockrooms and offices may require dedicated coverage rather than relying on the sales-floor access point.

Camera and loss-prevention infrastructure

UniFi Protect cameras can cover entrances, registers, aisles, stockrooms, loading areas, and parking. Storage planning reflects camera count, resolution, and retention needs.

PoE switching and cabling are sized for both cameras and access points.

Guest and staff WiFi

Separate staff and guest networks improve organization and reduce exposure. Guest bandwidth can be controlled so it does not affect payment systems or business operations.

Staff devices can receive access appropriate to their role.

Multi-location consistency

Businesses with multiple locations benefit from consistent naming, equipment standards, documentation, and remote visibility.

Each location still requires design based on its floor plan and construction.

Frequently asked questions

Can POS systems be isolated from guest WiFi?

Yes. They can be placed on separate networks with appropriate policies.

Can cameras and WiFi share the same switches?

Yes, when PoE capacity, ports, uplinks, and traffic are sized correctly.

Can you support multiple locations?

Yes. Standards can be repeated while each site receives its own coverage and infrastructure plan.

Do you install after hours?

Scheduling depends on the project and business needs and can be discussed during planning.
