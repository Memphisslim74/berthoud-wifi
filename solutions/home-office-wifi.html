Why home-office performance is different

Work traffic is sensitive to latency, packet loss, and brief disconnects. A connection can show high download speed and still perform poorly during video meetings or remote desktop sessions.

Other household activity, including streaming, gaming, cameras, and cloud backups, can expose weaknesses in the router, switching, WiFi coverage, or internet upload capacity.

Wired versus wireless workstations

A wired Ethernet connection is the most predictable option for a permanent desk. When wiring is not practical, a well-placed access point with stable backhaul can provide excellent service.

We evaluate the office location, cable pathways, device requirements, and whether phones, printers, docks, or other equipment also need connections.

Separate work and personal devices

A dedicated network can help organize work devices separately from guest, IoT, and personal equipment. This is especially useful for households with many smart devices or where an employer has specific security expectations.

Network separation is configured only where it provides practical value. The design should remain understandable and supportable.

Power and outage planning

A UPS can keep the gateway, modem or fiber terminal, switch, and access point online through short power interruptions. This can prevent brief outages from dropping calls or forcing equipment to reboot.

Backup internet options may also be considered for users whose work cannot tolerate provider outages.

Installation and testing

We test from the office under realistic conditions, verify wired links, review WiFi roaming, and confirm that the network performs while other household devices are active.

The completed setup is labeled and explained so the homeowner knows what equipment supports the office.

Frequently asked questions

Is Ethernet always required for a home office?

No, but it is the most predictable option for a fixed workstation. A properly designed WiFi connection can also work well.

Can you create a separate work WiFi network?

Yes. UniFi can provide separate networks and policies where that improves organization or security.

Can you help with video-call problems?

Yes. We examine coverage, interference, wired links, gateway performance, upload capacity, and other causes of unstable calls.

Do you install battery backup?

We can include UPS planning for the modem or fiber terminal, gateway, switches, and access points.
