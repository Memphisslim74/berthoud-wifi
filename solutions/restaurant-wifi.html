Restaurant WiFi and Network Installation

Reliable restaurant WiFi, point-of-sale connectivity, guest networks, cameras, and UniFi infrastructure.

Request a QuoteInstallation Process

Restaurants depend on the network for point-of-sale systems, printers, payment devices, online ordering, music, cameras, staff devices, and guest WiFi. These systems need structured connectivity and separation.

Critical systemsPOS, payment terminals, printers, online orders, and back-office devices.
Guest accessSeparate WiFi with appropriate limits and isolation.
Coverage areasDining room, kitchen, patio, office, storage, and curbside areas.
InfrastructureGateway, PoE switches, access points, cameras, cabling, and UPS.

Separate operational and guest traffic

Point-of-sale systems and payment devices should not share an open network with guests. Staff, POS, cameras, music, printers, and visitors can use separate networks with appropriate communication rules.

This structure improves troubleshooting and reduces unnecessary access between systems.

Coverage in difficult spaces

Kitchens contain stainless steel, equipment, refrigeration, tile, and dense walls that change radio behavior. Dining areas, patios, and back offices may require different access-point placement.

We test representative areas rather than assuming one access point can cover the entire restaurant.

Point-of-sale reliability

Wired connections are preferred for fixed POS stations and printers where practical. WiFi coverage is designed for handheld terminals, tablets, and mobile devices.

Gateway, switching, and UPS planning can reduce interruptions caused by local equipment failure or brief power events.

Guest WiFi experience

A guest network can provide straightforward access while limiting bandwidth and preventing access to private devices. Captive portals or branded welcome pages may be considered.

The guest experience should not interfere with ordering, payment, or operational traffic.

Cameras and remote management

UniFi Protect cameras can cover entrances, cash-handling areas, dining rooms, parking, storage, and exterior spaces. Recorder capacity and retention are planned around camera count and resolution.

Central management provides visibility into the network and connected equipment.

Frequently asked questions

Can guest WiFi be separated from POS systems?

Yes. Separate networks and policies can keep guest devices away from operational systems.

Should POS stations be wired?

Fixed stations and printers are generally more predictable when wired, while handheld devices may use WiFi.

Can you cover an outdoor patio?

Yes. Weather-rated access points can serve outdoor dining areas.

Can cameras use the same UniFi system?

Yes. UniFi Protect cameras can be integrated with the network infrastructure.
