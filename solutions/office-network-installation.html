Design around business workflows

We review user count, devices, internet service, conference rooms, cloud applications, VoIP, printers, cameras, and expected growth before selecting hardware.

Coverage is tested where staff work rather than only near the network closet.

Wired and wireless balance

Fixed workstations, phones, printers, and conference-room systems often benefit from wired connections. Mobile devices and flexible workspaces require well-designed WiFi.

PoE switching can power access points, phones, cameras, and access devices from a central location.

Separate network functions

Staff, guests, cameras, phones, printers, and building devices can use separate networks where appropriate. This improves visibility and reduces unnecessary communication.

Policies remain practical so business applications continue to work.

Conference rooms and video meetings

Video calls are sensitive to packet loss, latency, congestion, and brief interruptions. We review coverage, wired options, switching, internet upload capacity, and traffic patterns.

Critical room systems can be given more predictable connections.

Handoff and future growth

The installation is labeled and documented. Switch capacity, uplinks, rack space, and access-point placement can allow future staff, cameras, or office expansion.

Remote management can reduce the time required to diagnose later issues.

Frequently asked questions

How many access points does an office need?

The number depends on floor plan, construction, users, devices, and capacity—not only square footage.

Can guest WiFi be separated from staff devices?

Yes. UniFi supports separate guest and staff networks.

Can phones and cameras use PoE switches?

Yes, when the switch provides the required ports and PoE capacity.

Can existing cabling be reused?

Existing cabling can be tested and reused when it supports the final design.
