Residential Networking

Better WiFi starts with the layout of the home—not another extender.

Dead zones, unstable video calls, buffering, slow gaming, disconnected cameras, and weak garage or patio coverage are usually design problems. We evaluate the internet handoff, floor plan, building materials, available cabling, and the devices that actually need service.

Depending on the property, we may recommend proven UniFi U6 access points, newer U7 models, wired backhaul, managed switching, guest networks, IoT separation, and outdoor coverage. We do not push WiFi 7 when a U6 model is the better fit.

Common Questions

Before you request an installation.

Do you only install WiFi 7 equipment?

No. We install both UniFi U6 and U7 models. A proven U6 access point may be the better choice depending on coverage, radio capability, client devices, internet speed, and cost.

Can you connect a barn, shop, or detached garage?

Yes. Depending on distance and the property, the solution may use a point-to-point wireless bridge, fiber, Ethernet, remote switching, and dedicated access points.

Do you install cameras and access control?

Yes. We install UniFi Protect cameras, recorders, doorbells, and select UniFi Access readers, hubs, and intercom systems.

Can you improve an existing network instead of replacing everything?

Yes. Existing equipment and cabling can be reused when they remain appropriate. The goal is a dependable system, not unnecessary replacement.

Do you work with homes and businesses?

Yes. Projects include homes, offices, shops, churches, restaurants, studios, rural properties, and other Northern Colorado locations.
