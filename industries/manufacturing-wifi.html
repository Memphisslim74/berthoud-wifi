Industrial RF conditions

Metal, machinery, electrical noise, walls, and moving equipment change wireless performance.

Coverage must be tested around the actual production environment.

Separate operational systems

Production devices, staff, guests, cameras, and office systems may require separate networks.

Rules should support operations without unnecessary exposure.

Fiber and remote switching

Large facilities may use fiber between network areas and PoE switches near access points or cameras.

Environmental conditions affect enclosure and equipment choices.

Cameras and loading areas

Production floors, entrances, loading docks, yards, and storage areas may need camera coverage.

Storage and uplinks are sized for camera count and resolution.

Documentation and uptime

Labels, diagrams, UPS protection, and spare capacity reduce troubleshooting time.

Maintenance windows and operational constraints are included in planning.

Serving Northern Colorado

Berthoud WiFi is based in Berthoud and serves Loveland, Fort Collins, Longmont, Erie, Boulder, Windsor, Johnstown, Timnath, Mead, Frederick, Firestone, Wellington, Greeley, and nearby communities.

Frequently asked questions

Can WiFi work around metal machinery?

Yes, but access-point placement and testing are critical.

Can you connect separate buildings?

Yes, using fiber or wireless bridges.

Can cameras share the network?

Yes, with proper switching and segmentation.

Do you support industrial control systems?

We provide network infrastructure and coordinate with the system vendor where needed.
