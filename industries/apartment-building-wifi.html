Common areas and management

Lobbies, offices, gyms, pools, clubhouses, and maintenance areas may need managed connectivity.

Tenant internet services require a different scope and provider strategy.

Cameras and access

Entrances, parking, mail areas, hallways, and common spaces may use UniFi Protect and Access.

Privacy, legal, and property policies must guide placement.

Building backbones

Fiber and managed switching can connect floors, buildings, and network rooms.

Intermediate closets need power, ventilation, and service access.

Guest and staff networks

Property staff and visitors can use separate networks with appropriate policies.

Operational devices should not share unrestricted guest access.

Scalability

Multi-unit environments need clear documentation, ownership, monitoring, and expansion planning.

The design should avoid consumer equipment scattered throughout the property.

Frequently asked questions

Do you provide internet service to every apartment?

Berthoud WiFi provides network design and installation; bulk tenant internet requires provider and property-specific planning.

Can cameras cover parking areas?

Yes, with appropriate placement and connectivity.

Can access control be integrated?

Yes, on suitable doors and entry points.

Can multiple buildings be connected?

Yes, through fiber or dedicated wireless bridges.
