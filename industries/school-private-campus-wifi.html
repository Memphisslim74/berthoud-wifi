Managed WiFi, classroom connectivity, cameras, guest access, and network infrastructure for private schools and small campuses.

Classroom density

Many student and staff devices can connect simultaneously, creating capacity demands beyond simple coverage.

Access points and channels must be planned for density.

Staff, student, guest, and device networks

Different groups may require separate access and policies.

Printers, cameras, displays, and IoT devices should be organized appropriately.

Cabling and switching

PoE switching, fiber uplinks, network closets, and classroom cable routes support the wireless system.

Large buildings may need multiple closets.

Cameras and entrances

Entrances, hallways, parking, and common spaces may use cameras and controlled access.

Policies and legal requirements should guide deployment.

Documentation and support

Schools need clear ownership, role-based access, labels, and diagrams.

The network should remain supportable by staff or an IT provider.

Frequently asked questions

Can student and staff WiFi be separate?

Yes.

Can you support high device counts?

Yes, with density-focused design and sufficient internet capacity.

Do you install cameras and access control?

Yes, for suitable private-school environments.

Do you provide content filtering?

Network infrastructure can integrate with filtering solutions, but policy and service selection are separate decisions.
