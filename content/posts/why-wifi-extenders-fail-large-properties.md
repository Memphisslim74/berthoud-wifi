A WiFi extender can look like the quickest way to fix a dead zone. On a larger home or multi-building property, it often adds another weak wireless hop instead of solving the actual coverage problem.

## The underlying problem is usually placement or backhaul

Reliable WiFi starts with access points placed where devices actually need coverage. Those access points also need a dependable connection back to the network. Wired Ethernet, fiber, or a dedicated building bridge usually performs better than repeating an already weak signal.
