Platform similarities

Both offer centrally managed gateways, switches, and access points for home and small-business environments.

Product selection, interface, ecosystem integration, and support approach differ.

Management

UniFi uses the UniFi application ecosystem, while Omada uses its controller platform.

Both can provide VLANs, guest networks, managed switching, and multiple sites.

Camera and access ecosystem

UniFi integrates Protect cameras and Access control in the broader platform.

Omada networking is separate from TP-Link’s other product families.

Hardware selection

Both offer multiple access points and switches. Specific radio, PoE, uplink, and availability requirements should drive selection.

Neither brand should be chosen solely from a feature checklist.

Local installation considerations

Berthoud WiFi specializes in UniFi, including networking, cameras, access, bridges, and management.

Customers already standardized on Omada may prefer to extend their existing environment.

Serving Northern Colorado

Berthoud WiFi is based in Berthoud and serves Loveland, Fort Collins, Longmont, Erie, Boulder, Windsor, Johnstown, Timnath, Mead, Frederick, Firestone, Wellington, Greeley, and nearby communities.

Frequently asked questions

Is Omada cheaper?

Pricing varies by model and project. Total cost includes gateways, switching, PoE, cabling, and installation.

Can both support VLANs?

Yes, with compatible managed equipment.

Which has better camera integration?

UniFi has a more unified Protect and Access ecosystem.

Do you install Omada?

Berthoud WiFi is UniFi-first.
